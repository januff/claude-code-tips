## Project Principles

Articulated across multiple sessions, especially 2026-03-20. These guide all decisions:

### 1. Freshness as the Central Metric
The project's value is measured by how current it is, not how complete. Every auto-generated section should show its last-updated timestamp. Staleness is the most important signal — both to us and to any visitor evaluating the project's health.

### 2. Watch-Then-Adopt (Not Adopt-Immediately)
Don't implement community solutions the moment they appear. Watch the trend of solutions over a 2-4 week window, then adopt when the approach has stabilized and we genuinely need it. This applies especially to memory, continual learning, MCP integrations, and session management — all areas where best practices shift faster than we can implement them. Build periodic "review conferences" and "decision conferences" into the workflow to create space between observation and action.

### 3. Never Reinvent Without Searching First
Before building any solution, exhaustively search our own database of 562+ community tips, then the web. Expert evaluators will ask: "Why didn't you use the existing community/first-party solution?" We must always have an answer — either we adopted it, or we have a documented reason why we didn't. This is the research-first heuristic applied to our own knowledge base, not just external resources.

### 4. Memory Quality = Session Transition Quality
Every new session is a test of how well we're implementing memory. The quality of reconstitution — how quickly and accurately a new instance gets up to speed — is the truest measure of the project's success. The goal is something between "quick-start bootstrap with essentials" and "unabridged full context." This will take 8-10 iterations minimum to stabilize. Each transition is data.
